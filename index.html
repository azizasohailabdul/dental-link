How it works
Three steps from pain to appointment
STEP 01
Search by what you need

Name the procedure, enter your ZIP, and tell us how you pay. We match you across hundreds of NYC practices so you never have to call every office blindly.

STEP 02
See real costs, upfront

Practices list their actual self-pay price ranges. You see the number before you pick up the phone. No more calling five offices just to ask what a cleaning costs.

STEP 03
Connect and get seen

Call or request a visit directly. No referral maze, no insurance phone tree. Just you and a dentist who accepts your situation and your budget.

The problem we are solving

Why this had to be built

The scale
74 million Americans have no dental insurance
More than the population of France. Most are not poor enough for Medicaid but are not offered employer coverage. They fall through the gap completely.
New York City
8 million people and a fragmented dental map
Even with the highest dentist density in the country, patients in the Bronx and Brooklyn wait weeks because they cannot find who accepts them before giving up.
The confusion
Insurance EOBs are designed to be unreadable
CDT codes, UCR fees, annual maximums, waiting periods. Most patients cannot tell what their plan covers until the bill arrives. ToothBridge decodes it before you walk in.
Language barriers
Over 200 languages are spoken across NYC
For patients who do not read English, finding a dentist who speaks their language and accepts their payment method is a full-day project. ToothBridge translates everything.
The bridge
One search that cuts through all of it
Procedure, location, payment type. Three inputs. Then a real list with real prices from practices that will actually see you. That is the whole idea.

Why CDT codes matter

The American Dental Association publishes over 600 procedure codes. Insurance companies use them to approve or deny claims. A patient who knows D1110 is a routine cleaning can challenge a denial. One who does not, cannot.

The story behind this

I built this because I had to watch what happens when the system fails.

I am a pre-dental student and full-time dental assistant at Expert Dental in New York City, working across two Manhattan clinics focused on oral surgery and endodontics. I graduated from CUNY Hunter College with a Bachelor of Arts in Biology and a minor in Sociology. That pairing was deliberate. I wanted to understand human systems, not just human anatomy.

"The patient who could not get care was not lacking a dentist willing to help. They were lacking information. That gap is a design problem. I decided to design something."

What I have witnessed from the dental chair, across thousands of patient interactions, is that the most painful part of a dental emergency is often not the tooth. It is the moment a patient realizes their insurance will not cover what they need, that the office does not accept their plan, or that the cost is simply beyond what they can figure out standing in a hallway in pain.

My future aspiration is to become a dentist myself. Through dental school, I aim to develop the clinical foundation to practice community-focused oral surgery, with a long-term goal of working in underserved urban communities where access barriers are highest. I want to become the kind of dentist who treats the patient in front of them and builds systems that reach the ones who never made it through the door.

ToothBridge is my first attempt at that second part.
